CMTS Issues Proclamation on Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion (DEI).

Posted On: July 03, 2021

At the CMTS Coordinating Board meeting held July 21, 2021, the Board approved a CMTS proclamation on diversity, equity, and inclusion (DEI). This document sets a foundation for interagency engagement related to DEI within the CMTS member maritime agencies and in support of DEI within the marine transportation system. The Coordinating Board further approved the establishment of a "DEI Task Team" led by the CMTS Executive Secretariat which has begun developing a workplan to implement the directive which includes engagement with the Ship Operations Cooperative Program at their annual fall event in September 2021.

Webinar: COVID-19 Vaccines for MTS Workforce

Posted On: February 04, 2021
Event Details: Wednesday, March 3, 2021

The U.S. Committee on the Marine Transportation System (CMTS) COVID-19 Working Group (C-19 WG) hosted a webinar for the maritime industry on “Covid-19 Vaccines for the MTS Workforce” on Wednesday, March 3, 2021, 2:00 – 3:30 p.m. EST. RDML Richard Timme, Assistant Commandant for Prevention Policy, USCG, and Ms. Lucinda Lessley, Acting Maritime Administrator (invited) provided opening remarks. CDR Alice M. Shumate, a CDC subject matter expert, addressed how vaccines work, vaccine safety, protection from vaccine, vaccine distribution, vaccine prioritization for merchant mariners and port workers, and CDC recommendations for the MTS workforce.

There was an ample opportunity for more than 170 industry, labor representatives and members of the marine transportation system workforce to ask questions. The C-19 WG hosted this vaccine webinar for the maritime industry in response to the multiple industry comments received during the virtual Industry Listening Session hosted by the C-19 WG on January 13. Refer to the event flier for details.

The C-19 WG serves as the CMTS forum for facilitating high-level interagency discussion, communication, and actions in support of the MTS under and after COVID-19. Please refer to https://www.cmts.gov/topics/working_group for the current C-19 WG activities and resources developed for the MTS.

Inventory Of Federal Communications Capabilities In The U.S. Arctic

Posted On: October 01, 2021

The Arctic IAT has developed an inventory of Federally-owned and operated communications systems at use in the U.S. Arctic. The inventory includes components from four different agencies, including the U.S. Coast Guard, NOAA, FAA, and U.S. Army Corps of Engineers. This effort was completed in response to a request by Alaskan Native Communities interested in Federal communications capabilities in the U.S. Arctic. The inventory, along with a response letter, was transmitted to the Alaskan Native Communities in July 2020. Annual Report

CMTS Establishes Interagency COVID-19 Working Group

Posted On: October 01, 2021

The U.S. Committee on the Marine Transportation System (CMTS) established the COVID-19 Working Group to facilitate interagency coordination in response to shared challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic. CMTS Coordinating Board Chair RDML Richard Timme and Maritime Administrator retired RADM Mark Buzby led the formation of the Working Group in recognition of the importance of supporting the efficient operation of the Marine Transportation System (MTS) under COVID-19. The Working Group provides a forum not only for the sharing of best practices between federal agencies, but also to engage with stakeholders and to learn from industry experiences on the front lines of the pandemic.

CMTS Hosts Webinar On Understanding Tribal Engagement In The U.S. Arctic

Posted On: September 03, 2020

Over 50 Federal agencies joined a webinar on August 25, 2020 hosted by The U.S. Committee on the Marine Transportation System (CMTS), "Understanding Tribal Engagement in the U.S. Arctic." Mr. Rob Edwardson, Tribal Liaison for the U.S. Coast Guard (USCG), District -17 (D-17) shared his lifetime of experience as a Native Alaskan (and retired USCG!) on the extent of USCG jurisdiction in Alaska and working with a Native population who have lived in the region for 10,000 years. Alaska is home to 229 Tribes of the 573 Tribes in the U.S. Alaskan Tribal history, organizations, and land management is very different than for Tribes in the "lower 48," requiring a sensitivity to the complicated state laws, jurisdictions, and traditions before starting even informal engagement. Mr. Richardson also shared the challenges for USCG in a D-17 area of responsibility that covers 3.9 million square miles; a jurisdiction that goes well out into the Pacific, into the Arctic; and borders Canada and Russia. For more information about USCG D-17 Tribal relations, contact Robert.H.Richardson@uscg.mil. For more information about CMTS work with Arctic maritime shipping, contact Geoffrey.Dipre@cmts.gov.

CMTS Issues M2M Report to the White House

Posted On: September 02, 2020

The first annual report on the status of the Federal Military to Mariner Initiative required under Executive Order (EO) 13860, "Supporting the Transition of Active Duty Service Members and Military Veterans into the Merchant Marine" is available for review.

The report was provided to the White House, Office of Trade and Manufacturing on 21 July 2020. The purpose of the report is to provide a status on the progress made by the Department of Homeland Security and the Department of Defense in five areas identified in the Executive Order. The report also highlights the many successes of the CMTS Military to Mariner Task Force to support the transition of active-duty sea service personnel to U.S. merchant mariner.

RDML Timme Appointed as CMTS Coordinating Board Chair

Posted On: August 6, 2020

As the CMTS celebrates 15 successful years as the Federal Government interagency maritime policy coordinating committee, it is pleased to announce that Acting Homeland Security Secretary Chad Wolf designated Rear Admiral Rich Timme, Assistant Commandant for Prevention Policy, US Coast Guard as the chair of the CMTS Coordinating Board. RDML Timme's term is for 12 months starting August 1, 2020.

Under the Admiral’s leadership, the CMTS will continue to work together to improve the overall awareness of the maritime domain, be responsible stewards of the marine environment, and promote a strong economy and a high quality of life for all Americans. The CMTS will also continue to support Executive Order 13840, Ocean Policy to Advance the Economic, Security, and Environmental Interests of the United States, to promote "the economic growth of coastal communities and ocean industries, which employ millions of Americans, advance ocean science and technology, feed the American people, transport American goods, expand recreational opportunities, and enhance America's energy security."

The CMTS is a Cabinet-level interdepartmental/interagency maritime policy coordinating committee chaired by Transportation Secretary Elaine L. Chao. As chair of the sub-cabinet Coordinating Board, RDML Timme will manage the day-to-day policy development, establish the annual work plan, and provide leadership to the CMTS Executive Secretariat Staff. More about the Admiral’s priorities may be viewed at: https://www.cmts.gov/about/leadership.

MTS-Federal Advisory Committee (FAC) list available June 2020

Posted On: August 6, 2020

We are pleased to announce that the CMTS compendium of Marine Transportation System-Related Federal Advisory Committees (FAC) has been updated from the July 2019 version.
The list includes 35 committees, of which 2 are maritime committees not subject to FACA rules. FACs were established in 1971 under Public Law 92-463, to ensure that recommendations provided by the various advisory committees is objective and accessible to the public. A FAC is a committee, board, or council, subject to rules related to the Federal Advisory Committee Act (FACA) that can advise or give recommendations to a sponsoring Federal agency or department. Fifty-one percent of FACs from the 2019 edition were updated, with 2 committees added to the matrix: the Committee and the National Geospatial Advisory Committee, and the National Maritime Interagency Advisory Group (NIAG).
The CMTS updates the MTS-related Federal Advisory Committee list to engage the agency Designated Federal Officers (DFO) and provide an appropriate forum to share priority MTS issue and opportunity for coordination among committees, as appropriate.

CMTS Coordinating Board Meets to Discuss Impact of COVID19 to the MTS

Posted On: June 2, 2020

Event Details: Wednesday, May 3, 2020

The CMTS Coordinating Board met at a virtual intersessional meeting to discuss and share observations on the impact of COVID19 to the MTS. Led by RDML Tim Gallaudet, Commerce Assistant Secretary of Oceans and Atmosphere and Deputy Administrator of NOAA, over 25 member agencies and directorates met to review the many and diverse impacts to the Marine Transportation System (MTS) from the COVID19 Pandemic. In particular, RDML Gallaudet called the session to discuss ways in which the CMTS partnership could enhance support to members during this very dynamic and ever changing time. The members also reported on the extraordinary efforts implemented to support the maintenance and recovery of the MTS, including:

NOAA's enhanced use of drones to support its ocean observation requirements under limited use of NOAA charting and research vessels;
US Coast Guard has implemented a number of regulatory relief advisories to support MTS operations such as allowance for merchant mariner credentials that may expire during COVID19 program office closures; Maritime Administration holds weekly calls with industry to receive first-hand reports of challenges to the MTS and works with Federal partners and industry to resolve them; Federal Maritime Commission has called Fact Finding 29 to identify information helpful to mitigating COVID 19 impacts on the supply chain and as it relates to FMC authorities; US Army Corps of Engineers has maintained the annual dredging program without interruption during the pandemic; A number of actions were proposed for the CMTS work plan for the Board’s consideration
